Privacy Policy — PAE Bird

Last updated: June 7, 2026

PAE Bird is a math productivity app available as a macOS application, a Microsoft Excel® add-in, and a Google Sheets™ add-on. This policy describes how PAE Bird handles your data across all three products.

Data Collection

PAE Bird collects no personal data of any kind — no names, email addresses, account information, or device identifiers are collected, stored, or shared by any PAE Bird product.

macOS App and Excel Add-in

  • All mathematical processing happens entirely on your device.
  • No data is sent to any server or third party.
  • No analytics, tracking, or telemetry are included.
  • No account or login is required.

Excel® Add-in

The PAE Bird Excel® add-in loads its math engine (a WebAssembly file) from GitHub Pages (salmon-benedict.github.io) when the add-in is first opened. This is a standard web request to download app code; no user data or spreadsheet content is transmitted. All calculations run locally inside Excel after the engine is loaded.

Google Sheets™ Add-on

The PAE Bird Google Sheets™ add-on works differently from the macOS and Excel® versions. When you use a PAE Bird formula (such as =PAE_SOLVE("x^2 - 5x + 6 = 0")), the math expression you type as the formula argument is sent to a PAE Bird API server hosted on Railway (railway.app) for processing. The result is returned to your spreadsheet.

  • Only the math expression string you provide as a formula argument is transmitted.
  • No spreadsheet content, cell data, or personal information is accessed or sent.
  • Expressions are processed in real time and are not stored or logged by the server.
  • No account or login is required.

Files

The macOS app reads and writes CSV files only when you explicitly load or save them using the app's file dialogs. These files remain on your device under your control. The Excel add-in writes results directly into your spreadsheet; no file data leaves your device.

Feedback Form

The optional feedback form opens Google Forms in your browser. Any information you choose to submit is governed by Google's Privacy Policy. Submitting feedback is entirely voluntary.
